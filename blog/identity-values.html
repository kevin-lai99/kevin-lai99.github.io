Identity Values

1.Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

It was during one of my part time jobs in a resturant where I forgot to charge a customer for their drinks. I contemplated on whether I should tell someone about this or just let it slide. In the end i told my manager about it and owned up to my mistakes. Thinking back, I think I made the right decision. Although the value of the drink is small, it is about being honest and responsible for my mistakes.

2.Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Growing up in a Chinese cultural environment, my values and identity have been deeply shaped by the traditions, philosophies, and societal expectations of my heritage. Principles such as respect for elders, filial piety, and the importance of education, have played a significant role in shaping my sense of duty and responsibility. I was taught to prioritize family and collective well-being over individual desires, reinforcing the idea that success is not just personal but also a reflection of my family's efforts. Our culture places great emphasis on perseverance and hard work, to endure hardships and remain resilient. This mindset has influenced my approach to challenges, teaching me to persist even in difficult times.

3.Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My key strengths include perseverance, adaptability, and curiosity. These qualities will support me throughout my learning journey. Growing up in a culture that values resilience, I have learned to push through challenges and setbacks. This determination will help me stay committed to my studies, even when I face difficulties or failures. My adaptability allows me to embrace different learning styles, technologies, and perspectives, ensuring that I can thrive in any educational setting. My curiosity drives me to ask questions, seek deeper understanding, and engage actively in my education. This will help me stay motivated and open to discovering new knowledge.

5.Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

During a group project in school, I was assigned to work with a team where one member was not contributing equally. While the rest of us were trying to complete the project on time, this team member often missed meetings and did not complete their assigned tasks. This created frustration and tension within the group. The strategies we've tried are open communication, task reassignment, and setting clear deadlines. These approaches somewhat worked, however some of the team members were unsatisfied as they would have to take on extra work to get all the work completed. Something I would do differently now is to set early expectations, meaning setting small deadlines on different parts of the project. This can make sure all members are on the same pace, meaning no members are significantly behind. I would also try encouraging collaborative work. Working in pairs or goups of three keeps the members more engages and motivated.
