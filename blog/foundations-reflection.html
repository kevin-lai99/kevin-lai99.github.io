Identity Values

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  • My ego resists feedback more than I thought
  • I associate my identity too closely with my performance
  • Vulnerability does not equal weakness

    • What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

    Values play a foundational role in both learning and programming. They influence your motivation and approach, whether you prioritize curiosity, collaboration, or integrity. In programming, your values shape how you write code, work with others, and make decisions that affect long-term maintainability and user trust.

    Empathy is essential for understanding others, whether you're learning alongside peers or building software for real users. It helps you consider different perspectives, communicate better in teams, and create solutions that are accessible, user-friendly, and genuinely helpful to the people who will use them.

    Self-awareness allows you to recognize your strengths and areas for growth. In learning, it helps you adjust your strategies and manage challenges like procrastination or perfectionism. In programming, it supports reflection, openness to feedback, and the emotional intelligence needed to grow and collaborate effectively.

    What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

    What surprised me most about the Human Skills learning was how deeply it impacted not just how I interact with others, but how I understand myself. I initially expected it to focus mainly on communication techniques or teamwork strategies, but it went much deeper, encouraging real self-reflection, emotional awareness, and personal growth. I didn't anticipate how much my mindset and behaviors would shift just by becoming more conscious of things like active listening, vulnerability, and how my ego shows up in daily interactions. It's been more transformative than I expected

    What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

    One of the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning was becoming aware of my own blind spots, especially around how I communicate and react under stress. It was uncomfortable at times to confront the ways my ego can get defensive or how I might unintentionally shut down open dialogue. Another challenge was practicing vulnerability in real conversations, particularly in group settings. Letting go of the need to always appear confident. It ultimately led to deeper, more authentic connections with others.

    Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

    I think a programming school focuses on Human Skills because technical skills alone aren't enough to thrive in the real world of web development. In the industry, success often depends just as much on communication, teamwork, and self-awareness as it does on coding ability. Developers work in teams, navigate feedback, handle pressure, and build products for real people. So being able to collaborate, listen, and empathize is essential. Human Skills help us become not just better coders, but better problem-solvers, teammates, and professionals.

    Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

    No, the time spent on Human Skills learning definitely doesn't feel like a waste. In fact, I think it's been essential. While programming practice is obviously important, the ability to collaborate, give and receive feedback, manage stress, and communicate effectively is what separates a good developer from a great one. In the real world, code is written in teams, under deadlines, with clients or users in mind. Without strong Human Skills, even the most technically skilled developers can struggle in those environments. The learning here has made me more self aware, more adaptable, and ultimately more prepared for the realities of working in tech.
