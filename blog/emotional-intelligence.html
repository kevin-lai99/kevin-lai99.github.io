Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the ability to understand, manage, and use your own emotions in positive ways to communicate effectively, empathize with others, overcome challenges, and defuse conflict. It also involves recognizing and influencing the emotions of others.

Difference between IQ and EQ

IQ is a measure of how well someone can think, solve problems, and understand complex ideas. It looks at things like logic, math skills, memory, and language ability. IQ is often linked to school performance and how well someone can handle technical tasks.

The main difference is that IQ measures thinking ability, while EQ measures emotional and social skills. IQ can help you do well in school or technical jobs, but EQ is important for building relationships, being a good team player, and becoming a strong leader.

Why is EQ important?

EQ is important because it helps you understand and manage emotions, both your own and other people's. This improves communication, reduces conflict, and strengthens relationships. It's useful in all areas of life. Personally, it helps me manage stress and make better decisions. Overall, EQ helps you stay in control, connect with people, and grow as a person.
